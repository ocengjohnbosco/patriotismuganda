b) Patriotism • According to dictionary definition ,the concept patriotism is derived from the word patriot,which is defined as a person who loves one’s country and who is ready to defend it against an enemy.
In Greek, where the word patria originates,the country is viewed in light of fatherhood, therefore patriotism is likened to the loyalty and love one would have for a father. Away from ordinary dictionary usage some philosophical study is presennted by Stephen Nathanson (1993) and defines patriotism as involving ;
• Special affection for ones own country
• A sence of personal identification
• Special concern for the well being of the country
• Wilingness to sacrifice to promote the countrys good.


In a nutshell, Nathanson is defining patriotism as love of ones country ,identification with it,and special concern for its well being and that of compatriots.

President Museveni has defined patriotism in terms of commitment to the core values of the nation. One ought to cherish and defend the sovereign existence of the country, the unity of the people, the economic endowments of the country which must benefit all the people hence requiring harmonious coexistence and respect for one another, democratic culture ,a love and respect for oneself, among others.

• It is important to note that many authors use patriotism and nationalism interchangably. In the 19thcentury , Lord Acton contrasted Nationality and patriotism as affection and instinct against a moral relation. In other words while patriotism is the awareness of our moral duties to the political community , Acton argues that Nationality is our connection with the race that is merely natural and physical

• In contrast another author , Elie Kedourie presented nationalism as a full fledged philosophical and political doctrine about nations as basic units of humanity with in which an individual can find freedom and fulfilment, while to Elie , patriotism is mere sentiment of affection for ones country.

• George orwell posits that nationalism is aggresive while patriotism is defensive attitudes. To him nationalism is about power and prestige for the nation ,while patriotism is a devotion to a particular place and a way of life one thinks best , but has no wish to impose it on others.

• To this extent , Orwell is arguing that country and nation are first run together, and then patriotism and nationalism get distinguished in terms of the strength of love and special concern one feels for it, the degree of ones identification with it.when there is no ill thoughts about others and hostile actions towards them, you have patriotism; but when they become unbrindled and cause one to think ill of others and act badly towards them, that is nationalism. So often you have patriots and nationalists , however , you may ask whether one can be both.

• Suffice to note finally that both concepts involve love of , identification with, special concern for a certain entity.

• The issue is that patriotism is often about the entire country, while nationalism is not necessarily the whole country but rather the nation , which sometimes is ethenic or merely a subnation/cultural entity. So when a country is not homogeneous, or when a nation lacks a country of its own , the two may contradict.

Politics
There are different perceptions about what politics is. On the surface, politics often associated with contemporary debates on governace, economic systems and power game.
while to a great extent the perceptions may be true, in this paper it is important to attempt an indepth understanding of the concept of politics,right from its historical genesis to its more materialist connotations

In the context of political economy,politics is centred on human and or societal processes.

While society is as old as man, politics arises only at the time when society disintengrates into classes and a state emerges. In otherwords politics is associated with state and classes. However,it is important to historically trace how and when politics emerges in society . Man as a societal being initially was very primitive, largely surviving on nature but with masterly of the use of fire ,many changes occured in the way man related to nature.1st becoming a cave dweller,later tools led to early domesticating animals and plants .More advance in the science of making tools/ technology, in turn led to more production,more surplus,and disintengration of earliest primitive communal society, into society of heirarchies and social classes leading to state formation.

• It is after state formation that politics became part of man’s science to manage societal problems arising out of class conflicts.Tools/weopons are eventually monopolised by a heirarchy in society to coerce the weaker class.

• This monopoly of tools of coercion is the origin of the army as a nucleus of the state . Politics therefore historically develops through the actions of the armed strong(war)

• It is war which eventually gives way to politics as a means of finding alternative means of intercourse other than bloody actions .

• Hence the saying that “Politics is war and war is politics by other means.”

• Politics in modern understanding has two levels ;ideological and practice .Therefore ,political education often addresses the idelogical aspects , which in turn shape practices ,otherwise called political activities/real politics.

• This particular concept is crucial in discussing patriotism , because knowledge about the distinction between a country, a nation can only be logically arrived at ,if one grasps the political economy aspects on evolution of mankind, which knowledge in turn leads to loving yourself as a member of mankind different from other natural or biological beings.


d) State
• In political Economy, a state is defined as an institutionalised machinery of authority that is exercised over a demarcated territory for purposes of maintaining cohesion , harmony,unity and economic well being of people in that territory.
• A state historically arises at the time society disintengrated into social classes , and therefore it is always an instrument of class-rule over other social classes.It is the contest of different social classes to control state power that constitutes the concept of politics.In history the nucleus of state formation has been the Army, and hence making the army as the chief pillar or component of the state.

f) Underdevelopment
According to Walter Rodney, underdevelopment does not mean absence of development ,but rather underdevelopment makes sense as a means of comparing different levels of development. Hence we talk of backwardness, when you compare with those economies that are ahead of others in terms of wealth and other economic indicators.
Underdevelopment also expresses particular relationship of exploitation , mainly one country exploiting another.It is significant to note that all countries named underdeveloped are and have been exploited by others.Todate most, if not all underdevelopment, is a function of imperialist exploitation.


g) Indentification of the enemy and allies
• Patriots are able to develop grand strategies or visions for their countries.
• A vision of a country is shaped by concrete realities on the ground and the most urgent and fundmental problems that require urgent attention. To this extent a vision arises from the estimates and appreciation made of the fundemental problems.In Africa and Uganda in particular the biggest bottleneck is backwardness with all its attendant manifestation,both social and economic.
• The vision of necessity becomes a country or society that is fully transformed from the state of backwardness hence one that is prosperous and developed. To realise such a vision the historic mission of all patriotic forces must be TRANSFORMATION. Therefore, the enemy or allies is determined in relation to the struggle for a transformed society.
• With a clear mission patriots must indentify allies as all those forces,individuals and other institutions that are positive and support the cause of transformation
• Likewise patriots must indentify,isolate,fight all those enemy forces who oppose or undermine the mission of transformation.
• For a long time the primary enemy of Africa has been foreign domination/imperialism .
• Imperialism operates both directly and indirectly through old colonial methods and neo-colonial tactics of using internal agents; unfair economic policies;promoting instability; bulkanisation and cultural domination.
