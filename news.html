THE National Secretariat for Patriotism Club (NSPC) on Friday asked the media to champion patriotism, saying it is an important ingredient in development.

“Whenever countries want to be great, patriotism is taken seriously. All the ancient civilisations emerged and survived on account of a deep spirit of patriotism among the citizenry,” said Lt. Col. Henry Masiko, the NSPC head.

He was speaking at a media dinner at the New Chong Qing Restaurant in Kampala.

Masiko’s deputy, Paul Odauk, noted that the platform offered by the media reaches a wide audience.

“We want to nurture a generation of Ugandans whose notions are intertwined with the future aspirations. This is based on national interests, and the common good.”

Odauk said the spirited campaign for patriotism was in cognisance of Uganda’s unpleasant history.

Kambarage Kakonge, the national guidance commissioner in the information ministry, said the Government was emphasising the training of journalists, hoping that the professionalism attained would address the country’s challenges.

John Kakande, the New Vision editor, said New Vision has and will always give a platform to champion patriotism.
