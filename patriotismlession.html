PATRIOTISM IDEOLOGICAL LESSONS

Time
2 hours
Topic
Introduction into patriotism
Content
• What is patriotism
• Why love Uganda
• Who is a patriot
• Core values of patriotism
Objectives
• Appreciate the role of patriotism in building Uganda
• To inculcate the spirit of patriotism into the students

Time
2 hours
Topic
Discipline and patriotic methods of work
Content
• What is patriotic discipline
• Elements of a patriotic discipline
• Behavior that is injurious to the common good
Objectives
• To analyze the essence of patriotic values, and how they impact on ones personal life
• Discuss how patriots can detect and counter ideas and actions that do not promote development and unity in the country

Topic
Geography of Uganda
Content
• Geographical location of Uganda
• Population of Uganda
• Uganda a resource
• Relief
• Vegetation
• Climate
• Wildlife and tourism potential
• The people of Uganda as a resource
Objectives
• To appreciate and cherish the abundant natural endowments of Uganda
• Appreciate that Uganda’s natural resources have not been fully harnessed
• To enable the patriots appreciate the need to guard our Ugandan resources jealously

Time
2 hours
Topic
History of Uganda
Content
• Early settlement
• The origin of Bantu, Luo, Nilohamites,
• Reasons for Migration
• Governance in pre-colonial Uganda
• Population linkages
• Regional production
Objectives
• Appreciate that the people of Uganda were making their progress following the natural path of social development
• To expose the fact that East Africa was a cradle of human civilization
• To appreciate the rich heritage of Uganda that can be built on to create a United people confident in their identity and abilities

Time
2 hours
Topic
Colonial history of Uganda
Content
• What is colonialism
• Origins of colonialism
• Motives of colonialism
• Methods of penetration by colonialists
• The Buganda agreement of 1900
Objectives
• To analyse colonial era in Africa in order to appreciate how it is linked to imperialist expansion by Europe.
• To analyse the effects of colonialism in Africa
• To examine the methods used by the colonialists in Africa
• To illustrate how colonialism led to the underdevelopment of Africa

• Characteristics of neo-colonies
• Uganda as a neo- colony (1962 to date
• Milestone events in Uganda’s post-independence history
Objectives
• To analyse the main characteristics of neo-colonialism
• Understanding the origins and manifestation of colonialism
• To examine the effects of neo colonialism on Uganda’s post- colonial development
